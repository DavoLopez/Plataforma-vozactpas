Resumen Rápido: El Chivato

REGLA DE ORO

Para transformar a pasiva necesitas siempre:
Sujeto pasivo + Verbo BE (en el mismo tiempo de la activa) + Participio Pasado (-ed / 3ª columna).

¿Cuándo usamos la pasiva?

  • No sabemos quién hace la acción o no importa (ej: The bank was robbed).
  • Damos importancia al objeto o resultado (ej: Gara's report was finished).
  • Textos científicos, formales o prensa escrita (ej: Antibiotics are used...).
  • Si queremos mencionar al agente que hace la acción, usamos by.

Transformación de Pronombres

Activa (Sujeto)

I • You • He • She • It • We • They

Pasiva (Agente)

me • you • him • her • it • us • them

Las 9 Estructuras Completas

1. Present Simple am/is/are + PP

Activa: study / studies • Pasiva: The classes are studied.

2. Present Continuous am/is/are being + PP

Activa: is/are studying • Pasiva: The song is being recorded.

3. Present Perfect have/has been + PP

Activa: has/have finished • Pasiva: Many trees have been planted.

4. Past Simple was/were + PP

Activa: painted / stole • Pasiva: The portrait was painted.

5. Past Continuous was/were being + PP

Activa: was/were repairing • Pasiva: Windows were being repaired.

6. Past Perfect had been + PP

Activa: had eaten / had lost • Pasiva: The cake had been eaten.

7. Future Simple will be + PP

Activa: will publish / will find • Pasiva: Results will be published.

8. Going to am/is/are going to be + PP

Activa: is going to build • Pasiva: It is going to be built.

9. Infinitive with to to be + PP

Activa: needs to do / wants to treat • Pasiva: Needs to be done.

Aprende desde cero

¿Qué es la Voz Activa y Pasiva?

En realidad, el concepto es igual que en español:

Voz Activa (Focus en el Sujeto)

El sujeto hace la acción directamente.

"Someone stole my bike."

(Alguien robó mi bici — Importa "quién")

Voz Pasiva (Focus en el Objeto)

El sujeto padece/recibe la acción.

"My bike was stolen."

(Mi bici fue robada — Importa "la bici")
¿Prefieres escuchar?
When we use active verbs, we normally focus on the person or thing that does the action. Someone stole my bike. However, when we use passive verbs, we focus more on the action or thing. My bike was stolen.

Fórmulas por Tiempos Verbales

Present Tenses / Tiempos de Presente
Present Simple Am/Is/Are + PP
A: I make a cake.
P: A cake is made.
Present Continuous Am/Is/Are being + PP
A: I am making a cake.
P: A cake is being made.
Present Perfect Have/Has been + PP
A: I have made a cake.
P: A cake has been made.
Past Tenses / Tiempos de Pasado
Past Simple Was/Were + PP
A: I made a cake.
P: A cake was made.
Past Continuous Was/Were being + PP
A: I was making a cake.
P: A cake was being made.
Past Perfect Had been + PP
A: I had made a cake.
P: A cake had been made.
Future & Others / Futuros y Otros
Future Simple Will be + PP
A: I will make a cake.
P: A cake will be made.
Going to Am/Is/Are going to be + PP
A: I am going to make a cake.
P: A cake is going to be made.
Infinitive + to to be + PP
A: I want to make a cake.
P: A cake wants to be made.
